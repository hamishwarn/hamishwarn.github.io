Problem 1

\[ \int x^x \, dx \]

\noindent Different integrals for $x^x$ (First four are in order of Desmos computational speed and accuracy for low number of terms). First two were found from working out, third was using a known derivation of the integral using the method of exhaustion, and fourth was using the definition of the integral using Riemann summation. \[\int x^x\,dx=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\sum_{k=0}^{n-1}\frac{-x^n\ln^k x}{(-n)^{n-k}k!}+C\] \[\int x^x\,dx=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\Bigg(\sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\frac{n^{k+1}(\ln{x})^{n+k}}{k!n!(n+k)}-(-n)^{-n}\Bigg)+C\] \[\int x^x\,dx=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\sum_{k=1}^{2^n-1}\frac{x(2^{-n}kx)^{2^{-n}kx} }{(-1)^{k+1}2^n}+C\] \[\int x^x\,dx=\lim_{n\to\infty}\frac{x}{n}\sum_{k=1}^{n}\Big(\frac{xk}{n}\Big)^{\frac{xk}{n}}+C\] \\ \[\int_{1}^{b}x^x\,dx=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\frac{n^{k+1}(\ln{b})^{n+k}}{k!n!(n+k)}\] \[\therefore\int_{1}^{e}x^x\,dx=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\frac{n^{k+1}}{k!n!(n+k)}\] \\ \[\int_{0}^{1} x^x\,dx=-\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}(-n)^{-n}\] \[\int_{0}^{1} x^{-x}\,dx=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}n^{-n}\] \newpage \noindent My derivation for the best series expression of \[\int x^x\,dx\] \[=\int_{0}^{x} t^t\,dt\] \[=\int_{0}^{x} e^{t\ln t}\,dt\] \noindent Using the Maclaurin series for $e^x$, \[=\int_{0}^{x} \sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\frac{(t\ln t)^n}{n!}\,dt\] \[=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\frac{1}{n!}\int_{0}^{x}(t\ln t)^n\,dt\] \[t=e^{\frac{-u}{n+1}},dt=\frac{-e^{\frac{-u}{n+1}}}{n+1}\,du\] \[\therefore\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\frac{1}{n!}\int_{0}^{x}(t\ln t)^n\,dt=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\frac{1}{n!}\int_{\infty}^{-(n+1)\ln x}\Big(e^{\frac{-u}{n+1}}\Big)^{n+1}\bigg(\frac{-u}{n+1}\bigg)^n\bigg(\frac{-1}{n+1}\bigg)du\] \[=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\frac{(-1)^n}{n!(n+1)^{n+1}}\int_{-(n+1)\ln x}^{\infty}e^{-u}u^n\,du\] \[=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\frac{(-1)^{n-1}}{(n-1)!n^{n}}\int_{-n\ln x}^{\infty}e^{-u}u^{n-1}\,du\] \noindent For readability purposes, let $-n\ln x=a$. For now, just consider the integral above. \[\int_{a}^{\infty}e^{-u}u^{n-1}\,du\] \[\textrm{IBP with }u=u^{n-1},dv=e^{-u}\] \[=e^{-a}a^{n-1}+(n-1)\int_{a}^{\infty}e^{-u}u^{n-2}\,du\] \[\textrm{Using IBP again for second time:}\] \[=e^{-a}a^{n-1}+e^{-a}a^{n-2}(n-1)+(n-1)(n-2)\int_{a}^{\infty}e^{-u}u^{n-3}\,du\] \[\textrm{Using IBP again for third time:}\] \[=e^{-a}a^{n-1}+e^{-a}a^{n-2}(n-1)+e^{-a}a^{n-3}(n-1)(n-2)+(n-1)(n-2)(n-3)\int_{a}^{\infty}e^{-u}u^{n-4}\,du\] \noindent Thus, after performing IBP a total of $n-1$ times, we get \[=e^{-a}a^{n-1}+e^{-a}a^{n-2}(n-1)+\cdots+e^{-a}a^{2}\frac{(n-1)!}{2}+e^{-a}a(n-1)!+(n-1)!\int_{a}^{\infty}e^{-u}\,du\] \[=e^{-a}a^{n-1}+e^{-a}a^{n-2}(n-1)+\cdots+e^{-a}a^{2}\frac{(n-1)!}{2}+e^{-a}a(n-1)!+e^{-a}(n-1)!\] \noindent Re-arranging back to front, and rewriting: \[=e^{-a}(n-1)!+e^{-a}a(n-1)!+e^{-a}a^2\frac{(n-1)!}{2}+\cdots+e^{-a}a^{n-2}\frac{(n-1)!}{(n-2)!}+e^{-a}a^{n-1}\] \[=\frac{e^{-a}(n-1)!}{0!}+\frac{e^{-a}a(n-1)!}{1!}+\frac{e^{-a}a^2(n-1)!}{2!}+\cdots+\frac{e^{-a}a^{n-2}(n-1)!}{(n-2)!}+\frac{e^{-a}a^{n-1}(n-1)!}{(n-1)!}\] \[=e^{-a}(n-1)!\bigg(\frac{a^0}{0!}+\frac{a^1}{1!}+\frac{a^2}{2!}+\cdots+\frac{a^{n-2}}{(n-2)!}+\frac{a^{n-1}}{(n-1)!}\bigg)\] \[=e^{-a}(n-1)!\sum_{k=0}^{n-1}\frac{a^k}{k!}\] \noindent This sum is also known as the incomplete gamma function. As $a_1=-n\ln x$, \[e^{-a}(n-1)!\sum_{k=0}^{n-1}\frac{a^k}{k!}=e^{n\ln x}(n-1)!\sum_{k=0}^{n-1}\frac{(-n\ln x)^k}{k!}\] \[=x^n(n-1)!\sum_{k=0}^{n-1}\frac{(-n\ln x)^k}{k!}\] \noindent Using this result, \[\therefore\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\frac{(-1)^{n-1}}{(n-1)!n^{n}}\int_{-n\ln x}^{\infty}e^{-u}u^{n-1}\,du=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\frac{(-1)^{n-1}x^n(n-1)!}{(n-1)!n^{n}}\sum_{k=0}^{n-1}\frac{(-n\ln x)^k}{k!}\] \[=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\sum_{k=0}^{n-1}\frac{(-1)^{n-1}x^n(-n\ln x)^k}{n^{n}k!}\] \[=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\sum_{k=0}^{n-1}\frac{(-1)^{n+k-1}x^n n^k\ln^k x}{n^{n}k!}\] \noindent For all values of $n$ and $k$, $(-1)^{n+k-1}=(-1)^{k-n-1}$ \[=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\sum_{k=0}^{n-1}\frac{(-1)^{k-n-1}x^n\ln^k x}{n^{n-k}k!}\] \[=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\sum_{k=0}^{n-1}\frac{-x^n\ln^k x}{(-n)^{n-k}k!}\] \noindent Thus arriving at our solution. \[\therefore\int x^x\,dx=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\sum_{k=0}^{n-1}\frac{-x^n\ln^k x}{(-n)^{n-k}k!}+C\] \\ \\ \noindent Similarly, we can also get \[\int x^{-x}\,dx=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\sum_{k=0}^{n-1}\frac{(-x)^n\ln^k x}{(-n)^{n-k}k!}+C\] \\ \\ \noindent Together, these results allow us to obtain the Sophomore integrals \[\int_{0}^{1}x^x\,dx=-\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}(-n)^{-n}\] \[\int_{0}^{1}x^{-x}\,dx=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}n^{-n}\]
